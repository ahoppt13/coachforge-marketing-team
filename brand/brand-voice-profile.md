# CoachForge AI — Brand Voice Profile

> The single source of truth every writing agent (Strategist, Ideator, Scripter, Head of Content) must obey. Derived from Brand Guidelines v1.0 (May 2026). If a draft violates anything here, it does not ship.

---

## 1. One-line identity
**Build your coaching business. Let AI do the heavy lifting.**

Digital products for working coaches (fitness, business, life) earning £2k–£20k/mo who want to scale without hiring a team. Founder-led, built by a working coach. Productised real workflows, not theory.

**Tone in three words: Confident. Considered. Direct.**

---

## 2. The two registers (pick by surface)

### Register 1 — Marketing voice
Earns attention from a stranger in-feed. Direct, confident, mildly punchy. Short sentences. Says what most coaches won't say out loud. Specifics over adjectives.

**Use for:** Facebook ads, Instagram captions, landing page headlines, sales emails, social posts driving traffic, paid creative, Reels/short hooks.

> Stop selling your time. The systems used by the top one percent of coaches — packaged into one download. No course. No upsell. Just the prompts, workflows, and templates that take you from drowning in admin to running a business.

### Register 2 — Product voice
Inside the products: calmer, instructive, patient without being patronising. Treats the reader as a peer, not a student. Assumes intelligence, respects time.

**Use for:** prompt packs, tutorials, course content, in-product walkthroughs, knowledge base, video scripts, onboarding emails after purchase.

> This prompt is best used after you've finished a client intake call. Paste the call transcript or your notes into Claude, run the prompt below, and you'll get a four-week programme outline tailored to the client's goals, constraints, and current capacity.

**Default for social content pipeline:** Register 1 for feed posts/hooks/captions/ads. Register 2 only for educational/tutorial scripts and post-purchase emails.

---

## 3. Voice principles (non-negotiable)
1. **State the value before the reason.** Lead with what the reader gets. Buried value is wasted value.
2. **Lead with the work, not the credentials.** Show the system first; mention who built it second.
3. **No false intimacy.** No "Hey friend!" Don't bring up the reader's feelings unprompted.
4. **Earn trust through specifics.** "Eight online clients in 90 days" beats "rapid growth." Numbers, timeframes, named tools, real workflows.
5. **Never sell on FOMO.** No countdown timers. No "only 7 spots left." No "price doubles at midnight."
6. **The work speaks.** Show, don't tell. Preview real product contents, real screenshots, real workflows.

## 4. Vocabulary

**USE:** systems · build out · leverage · working coach · real results · production · forge · substance · frameworks · show your work · specifics · practice

**BAN (never use):** game-changer · revolutionary · hustle/hustler · crush it · passive income · guru · hack · literally (as filler) · amazing · six figures (as a hook) · insane · manifest

---

## 5. Sentence shape

**DO:**
- Short. Direct. Confident.
- Sentence fragments for impact.
- Periodic sentences that land on the key word.
- Two-clause structures with a colon: like this.
- Active verbs over adjectives.

**DON'T:**
- Long, comma-laden sentences doing too much in one breath.
- "Trust me when I say…" openers.
- Rhetorical questions that don't earn the question mark.
- "What if I told you…" openers.
- Adjective stacks: "premium, elite, world-class".

---

## 6. Hard rules (auto-reject a draft if any are true)
- Uses a banned vocabulary word.
- Uses FOMO / fake scarcity / countdown pressure.
- Uses US English or $ pricing (brand is **UK English + £**).
- Opens with "Hey friend!" or fake intimacy.
- Claims quality instead of demonstrating it.
- Stacks adjectives instead of using specifics.
- Pretends AI replaces the coach (AI is leverage, not replacement).

## 8. Platform notes
- **Instagram first.** Carousel-driven. Every post starts on obsidian. Heavy amber type on dark. Real product screenshots are the dominant visual.
- Captions in Register 1 — but everyday feed posts are *not* full sales-page tone; save that for promo sequences.
- **Email:** plain-text first, no banner, weekly cadence, sign off "— CoachForge AI".
- **Gumroad:** Register 1 but tighter; buyers scan. Lead with deliverable → price → what's inside → proof.

## 9. Visual system (for the designer agent)
- **Colours:** Obsidian Navy `#070B14` (bg) · Deep Navy `#0B1628` (cards) · Navy Mid `#1A2F52` (borders) · Molten Amber `#E8930C` (primary accent/CTA) · Amber Gold `#F5A832` (hover) · Amber Glow `#FDD89A` (spark detail only) · Warm Ivory `#F2EDE4` (text on dark) · Slate `#6B80A3` (secondary text).
- **Pairing:** Obsidian + Molten Amber = primary. Warm Ivory on Navy for reading. Molten Amber on dark ONLY (never on white). One amber tone per composition. Amber Glow = single-point detail, never a fill.
- **Type:** Cormorant Garamond 600 for display/brand moments; DM Sans 400/600 for everything functional. Mixed case headlines, sentence-case buttons, ALL CAPS only for tracked eyebrow text.
- **Photography:** real working-coach + tool-focused shots only. **Never** stock, never generic AI imagery (glowing brains, circuit boards), never before/after transformations, never LinkedIn-premium.

---

## 10. Quick reference
**DO:** lead with substance · be specific (numbers, timeframes, screenshots) · founder track record as evidence · match register to surface · obsidian/amber/ivory · Cormorant for brand moments, DM Sans otherwise.

**DON'T:** guru words · FOMO/fake scarcity · stock photography · amber on white · third colour or third typeface · "Hey friend!"
